History of Indian Army

1776 - Formation

The Indian Army traces its origins to the armed forces of the East India Company, which eventually became the British Indian Army. The first company regiment was established in 1776.

1947 - Independence

After India gained independence on August 15, 1947, the British Indian Army was divided between India and Pakistan. The Indian Army became the military of the newly formed nation.

1950 - Republic Day

General K.M. Cariappa became the first Indian Commander-in-Chief of the Indian Army, replacing the last British Commander, General Sir Francis Butcher.

Present Day

Today, the Indian Army stands as one of the largest and most professional military forces in the world, committed to defending the nation's sovereignty and territorial integrity.

Major Wars Fought

1947-48

Indo-Pakistani War

The first war between India and Pakistan over the Kashmir conflict. Indian forces successfully defended strategic positions and secured significant territories.

Victory
1962

Sino-Indian War

Border conflict with China in the Himalayan regions. Despite initial setbacks, the Indian Army demonstrated remarkable resilience and learning.

Ceasefire
1965

Second Indo-Pakistani War

Major conflict over Kashmir where Indian forces successfully defended against Pakistani aggression and launched counter-offensives.

Victory
1971

Bangladesh Liberation War

India's decisive military intervention led to the creation of Bangladesh. One of the shortest wars in history with a clear victory.

Decisive Victory
1999

Kargil War

High-altitude conflict in Kargil-Drass sector. Indian forces successfully evicted infiltrators from strategic heights through Operation Vijay.

Types of Getting Commissioned

The Indian Army offers multiple entry routes for aspiring officers and soldiers. Each path is designed to identify and nurture leadership qualities while providing excellent career opportunities.

01

National Defence Academy (NDA)

Eligibility: 10+2 pass, unmarried male candidates (16.5-19.5 years)

Training: 3 years at NDA + 1 year at IMA

Description: The premier tri-service academy that trains cadets for all three services. Considered the most prestigious entry route.

Permanent Commission
02

Combined Defence Services (CDS)

Eligibility: Graduate, unmarried (19-24 years for IMA)

Training: 1 year at Indian Military Academy

Description: For graduate candidates who wish to join the armed forces through a competitive examination.

Permanent Commission
03

Technical Entry Scheme (TES)

Eligibility: 10+2 with PCM, unmarried male (16.5-19.5 years)

Training: 4-year B.E/B.Tech degree + officer training

Description: Direct entry after 10+2 for technical branches. Candidates complete engineering degree during training.

Permanent Commission
04

Short Service Commission (SSC)

Eligibility: Graduate (men & women), 19-25 years

Training: 49 weeks at Officers Training Academy

Description: Initial tenure of 10 years (extendable to 14 years). Option to apply for permanent commission.

Short Service
05

University Entry Scheme (UES)

Eligibility: Pre-final and final year engineering students

Training: 1 year at Indian Military Academy

Description: For engineering students from recognized universities. Selection through interview and medical examination.

Permanent Commission
06

Special Entry (JAG & AEC)

JAG: Law graduates (21-27 years)

AEC: Engineering graduates for Army Education Corps

Description: Specialized entries for legal and educational services in the Army.

Permanent Commission
